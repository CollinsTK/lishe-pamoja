# CHAPTER 7
## LIMITATIONS, RECOMMENDATIONS AND CONCLUSIONS

### 7.1 Introduction

In line with the general objectives of this study, this chapter outlines the limitations encountered during system development, provides recommendations for improvement, and presents the final conclusions drawn from the implementation and testing phases of the Lishe Pamoja platform.

### 7.2 Limitations

While the Lishe Pamoja system has been successfully implemented, certain limitations were identified during the development and testing phases:

**Platform Accessibility**: The application is currently implemented as a web-based platform, which may present accessibility challenges for users with limited internet bandwidth or older mobile devices without modern browser capabilities.

**Internet Dependency**: The system relies on a stable internet connection to access real-time features, including the interactive map for locating nearby food listings, M-Pesa payment processing, and instant order notifications. This can be a significant challenge in rural areas with poor connectivity infrastructure.

**Limited Vendor Participation**: The effectiveness of the surplus food redistribution model depends on the active participation of food vendors and businesses. Low adoption rates among potential sellers may restrict the variety and availability of surplus food listings for recipients.

**Logistics Coverage**: The platform's delivery capabilities are constrained by the number of registered logistics partners. Areas with limited courier participation may experience delayed deliveries or lack delivery services entirely.

**Payment Method Constraints**: While M-Pesa integration provides convenient mobile money transactions, the system currently lacks support for other payment methods such as bank cards, which may exclude users who prefer alternative payment options.

**User Onboarding Challenges**: Some users, particularly those unfamiliar with digital marketplace platforms, may find it difficult to navigate the subscription tiers, wallet system, and order management features, potentially affecting adoption rates among less tech-savvy demographics.

### 7.3 Recommendations

To enhance the functionality, accessibility, and impact of the Lishe Pamoja system, the following improvements are recommended:

**Broaden Vendor Recruitment**: Implement targeted outreach programs to encourage more restaurants, supermarkets, and food producers to join the platform. Offering incentives such as reduced subscription fees during initial onboarding or analytics dashboards showing potential revenue recovery could increase vendor participation.

**Improve User Onboarding**: Develop comprehensive tutorials, in-app guided tours, and contextual help features to assist new users in understanding the subscription model, wallet funding, and order placement processes. Consider implementing a simplified mode for first-time users.

**Enhance Offline Capabilities**: Introduce Progressive Web App (PWA) features with caching mechanisms that allow users to browse previously loaded food listings, view order history, and access essential information when internet connectivity is intermittent or unavailable.

**Expand Payment Options**: Integrate additional payment gateways such as card payments, bank transfers, and mobile banking apps to accommodate diverse user preferences and increase financial inclusion.

**Develop Native Mobile Applications**: Create dedicated Android and iOS applications to provide better performance, push notifications, and device-native features such as camera integration for vendor listing uploads and GPS optimization for logistics tracking.

**Implement AI-Driven Features**: Explore the integration of intelligent matching algorithms to suggest relevant surplus food listings based on user preferences, location, and purchase history. Predictive analytics could also help vendors optimize pricing and inventory management.

**Strengthen Logistics Network**: Establish partnerships with established courier and delivery services to expand coverage areas. Implement a rating and incentive system to motivate logistics partners and ensure service quality.

### 7.4 Conclusion

Lishe Pamoja successfully establishes a comprehensive digital platform that addresses the critical challenge of food waste and food insecurity by connecting surplus food vendors with recipients in need. The system enables real-time discovery of available food listings through an interactive map interface, facilitates secure transactions through M-Pesa integration, and provides efficient order management and delivery coordination across multiple user roles.

The implementation of distinct subscription tiers allows flexible access for different user types, from individual recipients browsing available food to vendors requiring sales capabilities and logistics partners managing deliveries. The unified dashboard design ensures a seamless user experience while accommodating the diverse needs of recipients, vendors, and administrators.

While the system achieves its primary objectives of reducing food waste and improving food access, addressing the identified limitations will further enhance its effectiveness and scalability. Future enhancements such as offline functionality through PWA capabilities, expanded vendor and logistics participation, improved onboarding experiences, multi-platform support through native mobile applications, and diversified payment options will contribute to a more robust, accessible, and user-friendly system.

Future research and development should focus on these areas to ensure that Lishe Pamoja remains a leading solution in surplus food redistribution and digital food security initiatives. The platform's architecture provides a solid foundation for continuous improvement and community impact, demonstrating the potential of technology to address real-world sustainability and social welfare challenges.
